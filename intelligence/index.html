Why Freqtrade's 49k Stars Are Misleading

  • • Monolithic package — strategy, exchange, persistence, RPC logic interleaved. No clean domain boundaries.
  • • No risk engine — risk management embedded in strategy callbacks, not a first-class component.
  • • No order book simulation — backtesting uses OHLCV only. No tick-level, queue, or latency modeling.

Freqtrade's 49k stars reflect its accessibility (one-command Docker, web UI, Telegram), not its engineering excellence.

Why Hummingbot's 18k Stars Are Misleading

  • • Core engine mixes strategy logic, order management, and market data — hard to extend.
  • • YAML-driven strategies limit expressiveness vs. code-driven approaches.
  • • Python GIL is a real bottleneck for multi-exchange, multi-strategy use cases.
